Inclusive Design 24 (#ID24) Information for presenters

A Free 24-Hour Online Community Event On Accessibility

Organized by The Paciello Group

Hello ID24 presenters!

As #ID24 rapidly approaches, we’ve put together a few bits and pieces for you to help you prepare for the big day.

  1. Please check the time of your talk once again. ID24 runs on UTC/GMT, so depending on where in the world you are, your talk may be at a different time (on Wednesday not Thursday for example). The ID24 website will show the time of your talk based on the time zone you’re currently in.
  2. Please be online and connected to your skype at least 15 minutes before your talk is due to start, and have your slides ready to go promptly on the hour. Your host will share your hangout URL with you at that time.
  3. Please keep to time. ID24 talks run for 45 minutes with a 5-minute question period at the end. We have to allow a 10-minute change-over between talks for the captioners and hosts. To keep ID24 running smoothly, we’ll be strict about finishing talks (including questions) inside of 50 minutes.

Hangouts

We use Google Hangouts via YouTube live. Because of how this is set up, the host will start the hangout at 10 minutes to the hour, and invite the other participants to join via a URL.

  • We recommend all speakers test their setup well in advance of next week. Billy Gregory will have open Hangouts running all week for speakers to join. If you’d like an invite, just ping Billy in the ID24 Skype channel.
  • Do not “Add” attendees from the Hangout URLs. Attendees/viewers will watch via YouTube or via the publicly published URLs on the ID24 site.

If you haven’t already, please:

  1. Let us have your Skype ID so we can add you to the presenter’s Skype chat channel. This is the place to ask questions and get help between now and the end of ID24. You’ll find members of the TPG ID24 team there, including thebillygregory and Leonie.Watson.
  2. If you are unfamiliar with Hangouts on Air, see YouTube Help: Introduction to live streaming.
  3. We’ll be asking people to use Twitter and the #ID24 hashtag to ask questions.
  4. We’ll be available right up until Wednesday evening (EST) to walk anyone through hangouts should you need help. Ping us either via email or on Skype and we’ll be happy to assist you.

Things to note:

  1. Google Hangouts does not play well with video content unless it is hosted on YouTube (it buffers and gets “choppy”). We strongly recommend avoiding video content in your presentation.
  2. Hangouts will be moderated by at least two people including 1 TPG team member. These teams will be responsible for fielding any questions on twitter and will ask them to you at the end of your talk. These moderators will be silent for the duration of your talk unless there is a reason for them to interrupt you (for example, if your screen stops sharing or there are connectivity issues)
  3. The TPG moderators will also be responsible for starting and stopping the broadcast. At the beginning of the session they will “Go live” and you can share your screen. They will then mute themselves and the floor will be yours!
